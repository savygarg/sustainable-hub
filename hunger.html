Hunger remains the leading cause of death worldwide, fueled by conflict, climate change, the pandemic, and deepening inequalities. These factors have driven a sharp rise in food insecurity, with 2.4 billion people globally experiencing moderate to severe food insecurity in 2022. Among them, 148 million children suffered from stunted growth, and 45 million children under the age of five were affected by wasting, highlighting the devastating impact of malnutrition. Hunger traps individuals in cycles of poverty, reducing their productivity, increasing their vulnerability to disease, and making it harder to improve their livelihoods. The crisis is expected to worsen, with over 600 million people projected to face hunger by 2030. Tackling hunger and malnutrition is crucial for achieving sustainable development and ensuring a future where everyone has access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food.



TARGETS :

By 2030, end hunger and ensure access by all people, in particular the poor and people in vulnerable situations

By 2030, end all forms of malnutrition, including, the internationally agreed targets on stunting and wasting in children under 5 years of age, adolescent girls, pregnant and lactating women and older persons.

By 2030, double the agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers, in particular women, indigenous peoples, family farmers, pastoralists and fishers.

By 2030, ensure sustainable food production systems and implement resilient agricultural practices that increase productivity and production, that help maintain ecosystems.

By 2020, maintain the genetic diversity of seeds, cultivated plants, domesticated animals and their related wild species, including through soundly managed and diversified seed and plant banks, as internationally agreed.

Increase investment, including through enhanced international cooperation, in rural infrastructure, agricultural research and extension services, technology development in order to enhance agricultural productivity in the least developed countries.

Correct trade restrictions and distortions in world agricultural markets, including through the parallel elimination of all forms of agricultural export subsidies and in accordance with the mandate of the Doha Development Round.

Adopt measures to ensure the proper functioning of food commodity markets and facilitate timely access to market, in order to help limit extreme food price volatility.
